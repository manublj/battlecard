üìå 3.8.2 Issues Sheet: Tracking Developing Events

Issue Cards provide a structured framework for tracking contradictions that unfold over time. Unlike standalone events, Issue Cards ensure that contradictions are analyzed through their motion, capturing sharpening, neutralization, and transformation as they develop.

The Issues Sheet tracks contradictions through a three-row structure:

  1. Row 1: Issue Title & Description ‚Üí Defines the core contradiction and struggle.
  1. Row 2: Event Timeline ‚Üí Tracks only factual updates of the situation (parent-child event linking).
  1. Row 3: Three-Column Stance Model ‚Üí Tracks ideological stances with multiple headlines, descriptions, and stance-revealing events.

By structuring issues in this way, Zoomout prevents historical fragmentation and ensures that contradictions remain central to analysis.

üìå 3.8.3 The Struggles Sheet: Tracking Organized Political Battles

Struggles as the Aggregation of Political Battles

The Struggles Sheet consolidates political battles across multiple events into structured categories, allowing users to track how contradictions manifest at different levels of struggle. Struggles are organized by:

  • Scale: Local, national, or global.
  • Form: Electoral, direct action, armed struggle, legal battles, etc.
  • Sector: Economic, political, social, cultural.

Each struggle consists of multiple Issue Cards, ensuring that struggles are framed in terms of contradiction motion rather than as abstract topics.

Struggles as a Link Between Events and Contradictions

Since struggles emerge from contradictions, the system ensures that every tracked struggle is:

  1. Rooted in material contradictions (e.g., labor disputes in a capitalist structure).
  1. Connected to ruling-class responses (Legal Containment, Repression, Narrative Manipulation, Co-optation).
  1. Structured dynamically, evolving through real-time updates in the Events Sheet.

By integrating struggles into the broader contradiction framework, the Struggles Sheet provides a historical-materialist view of political battles, ensuring that contradictions are not only tracked but actively analyzed.

üî• This sheet allows Zoomout to capture how contradictions become the terrain of organized battles, shaping history. üöÄ

üìå 3.8.8 Entities Sheet

Entities play a central role in contradiction motion, and Zoomout automates their detection to ensure accurate contradiction tracking. The Entities Sheet dynamically pulls data from event descriptions and external sources to link political actors to relevant events.

Automated Entity Detection System

üöÄ NER (Named Entity Recognition) Model Integration

  • Extracts organizations, movements, and state actors from event descriptions.
  • Cross-references entities with past event data for accuracy.

‚úÖ How It Works:
1Ô∏è‚É£

2Ô∏è‚É£

3Ô∏è‚É£

üìå Auto-Linking Entities to Contradictions

  • The system categorizes each entity based on its role within contradictions:
    • Primary Actors ‚Üí Directly engaged in struggles (e.g., labor unions, revolutionary groups).
    • Secondary Actors ‚Üí Indirectly involved but influential (e.g., NGOs, think tanks, academic institutions).
    • Ruling-Class Institutions ‚Üí State bodies, corporate networks, and media structures that shape contradiction motion.

üî• Example:

    • Student Federation (Primary Actor) ‚Äì Organizing the protest.

üìå Linking Entities to Their Historical Role in Contradictions

üìå 3.8.9 Timeline Sheet

Timelines ensure that contradictions are mapped within historical struggles rather than being treated in isolation. Events can be linked to multiple timelines based on historical, economic, and class-based developments.

  • Example 1: The Indian Emergency (1975-77) is linked to both the Fascism vs. Democracy contradiction and the State vs. People contradiction.
  • Example 2: The Agrarian Crisis in Punjab today connects to historical peasant struggles from the 1980s and earlier.

Tracking contradictions over time is essential to understanding how political struggles evolve. Some contradictions escalate rapidly, while others re-emerge cyclically over decades. The Timeline Table helps structure these developments by:

  • Linking related events into historical struggles (e.g., multiple waves of farmers' protests).
  • Identifying trends in contradiction sharpening or neutralization over time.
  • Helping users track contradiction resurgence across different eras and political shifts.

This mapping ensures that contradictions are studied in their full historical context rather than as isolated events.

By using timelines, Zoomout enables users to see not just isolated events, but the larger historical forces shaping contradictions.

4.1 Core Frontend Architecture

The frontend of Zoomout is the primary interface for interacting with the system, visualizing contradictions, tracking struggles, and analyzing historical motion. It needs to balance data density, clarity, and usability while maintaining a rigorous materialist approach. This section outlines:

  • How frontend retrieves and renders data ‚Üí Interfacing with the backend via APIs, database calls, and caching mechanisms.
  • How events, contradictions, struggles, and structures are displayed dynamically ‚Üí Ensuring seamless transitions between different analytical layers.
  • The role of each core page in the system ‚Üí Explicitly defining functionality to prevent feature bloat and scope creep.

At its core, the frontend is not just a UI‚Äîit is the visualization layer of the entire historical materialist engine.

4.2 Core Pages & Their Functionality

Each page in Zoomout serves a specific role in analyzing historical motion, contradictions, and struggles. Below is a breakdown of each core page and its function.

4.2.1 Reporting Page

  • Primary function: This is the data entry & review hub, where users log events and analyze them.

The Reporting Page serves as the core event feed, displaying all recorded events in reverse chronology. It integrates different manifestations of events:

  • One-Off Events ‚Üí Displayed as individual tiles.
  • Event Timelines ‚Üí Events linked via parent_id & update_id are grouped.
  • Issue Cards ‚Üí When an event escalates into an ongoing issue, it is structured as an Issue Card, displaying its stance-based event timeline.

The floating "+" button on this page opens the Event Form, which writes data to the Events Sheet.

  • Purpose: Ensures every logged event immediately integrates into the broader analysis framework.

4.2.2 Wiki Page

The Wiki Page serves as the entity database, displaying entities as profile cards, categorized by entity type (Organizations, Political Parties, Movements, etc.).

  • Clicking a profile card reveals the entity‚Äôs history, stance-taking events, and timeline of actions.
  • The floating "+" button triggers the Entity Form, which writes data to the Entities Sheet.

Entity Profile Cards:

  • Capture historical stances, contradictions, and power dynamics.
  • Track how entities respond to contradictions over time.

  • Purpose: This is the archival layer of Zoomout‚Äîensuring entities and struggles are not just recorded but contextualized.

4.2.3 Issues Page

  • Primary function: Displays Issue Cards, which group related events into structured contradictions.

The Issues Page displays Issue Cards in reverse chronology. Each Issue Card contains:

  • Issue Title & Description (derived from the initiating event).
  • Event Timeline of the Issue (linked events developing the situation).
  • Three-Column Stance Model (stances of different entities, dynamically updated from events).

The floating "+" button on this page triggers the Issue Form, writing data to the Issues Sheet.

  • Purpose: Provides a dialectical breakdown of developing contradictions.

4.2.4 Timeline Page

  • Primary function: Displays historical motion through interactive timelines.

The Timeline Page enables users to:

  • View Core Element Timelines ‚Üí Entities, Struggles, Structures, Contradictions.
  • Track user-defined timelines (timeline_id).
  • Search & Filter Timelines based on entity, struggle, contradiction, or structure.

The floating "+" button allows users to create a new mapped timeline, writing data to the Timelines Sheet.

  • Purpose: This is where motion over time is explicitly visualized.

4.2.5 Structures Page

  • Primary function: Displays economic, political, social, and cultural structures‚Äîthe material basis shaping contradictions.
  • How it works:
    • Structures define the conditions within which contradictions emerge.
    • Users can track how structures evolve over time and how struggles shape or reinforce them.
  • Purpose: Ensures historical materialism is explicit in the UI by linking contradictions to their structural context.

4.2.6 Struggles Page

  • Primary function: Organizes struggles by scale (local, national, global), form (electoral, direct action, armed struggle), and sector (economic, political, cultural).
  • How it works:
    • Users can see how struggles develop over time.
    • Struggles connect dynamically to contradictions and events, preventing isolated analysis.
  • Purpose: Ensures struggles are not just tracked as events but as ongoing processes.

4.2.7 Contradictions Page

The Contradictions Page functions as a dialectical engine, displaying Contradictions as cards, emphasizing:

  • Historical motion of contradictions (Latent ‚Üí Developing ‚Üí Sharp ‚Üí Explosive).
  • Explicit links to Structures, Struggles, and Ruling-Class Management of Contradictions.

The floating "+" button on this page triggers the Contradiction Form, writing data to the Contradictions Sheet.

  • How it works:
    • Displays ruling-class management of contradictions ‚Üí Legal Containment, Repressive Control, Narrative Manipulation, Co-optation.
    • Contradictions link dynamically to structures, struggles, and timelines.
  • Purpose: Ensures contradictions are not passively logged but are actively analyzed in motion.
